Who I am

Former Law student turned Front-end Developer

I graduated university in 2018 with a degree in political science, with the intention of going to law school in the Bay Area. It was during my first year in law school that I decided to make the career transition to tech. After completing my first year in law school, I took up an internship for the summer. However, during my internship, I learned that the legal profession was not for me. So after I completed my internship, I did not return to law school and sought different fields for my professional career.

After months of research and going from job to job, I ended up temping for a primary care medical start up in San Francisco. I intended to step into tech by working at the start up in a non-tech role with the intention of transferring into software engineering within the same organization. In this role, albeit not a tech role, I observed the software development life cycle and how innovative ideas for new user applications go from a bullet on a white board to application features. Although, I had already made my decision to commit to changing my career to tech, witnessing the development cycle and being a part of a dynamic and collaborative startup culture, sealed the deal for me!
